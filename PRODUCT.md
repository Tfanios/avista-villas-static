# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Avista serves high-end travelers, families, small groups, and travel planners evaluating private villa stays in Vourvourou, Sithonia. They are comparing places from a distance and need to understand the houses, the setting, privacy, capacity, atmosphere, and whether the experience fits their group.

## Product Purpose

The site presents Avista as a private two-villa collection in Halkidiki and guides visitors toward an enquiry. Success means visitors quickly understand the difference between Avista Villa and Avista Private Resort, feel the privacy and place through imagery, and trust that the stay will be calm, considered, and family-ready.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, family, private.

The voice should feel calm, specific, and hospitable. It should speak through concrete details: pines, water, gardens, terraces, bedrooms, pools, shade, meals, and the pace of a private stay. It should avoid loud sales language and generic resort promises.

## Anti-references

No named anti-references were provided. Pattern-level anti-references:

- Generic booking-engine layouts that reduce the villas to inventory.
- Loud beach-club aesthetics, neon vacation energy, and overproduced resort spectacle.
- Anonymous stock-travel imagery or copy that could describe any villa in Greece.
- Crowded card grids, template-like icon sections, and repeated small uppercase labels above every section.
- Corporate travel-funnel language that makes enquiry feel transactional.

## Design Principles

- Lead with place and privacy. The first impression should make Vourvourou, the sea, the pines, and exclusivity immediately legible.
- Make the two-house choice clear. Visitors should understand which villa fits their group without hunting through the page.
- Let imagery do serious work. Photos should carry trust, atmosphere, scale, and material detail.
- Keep the pace unhurried. Spacing, motion, and copy should feel measured rather than busy.
- Make enquiry feel personal. Calls to action should feel like starting a considered conversation, not entering a checkout flow.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Maintain strong text contrast, keyboard-accessible navigation and modals, visible focus states, descriptive link text, useful image alt text, and reduced-motion alternatives for animation. The site should remain understandable for guests comparing villas on mobile devices, slower connections, and bright outdoor screens.
